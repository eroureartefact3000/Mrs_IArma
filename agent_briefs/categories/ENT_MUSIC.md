## Native-to-category check

Failure mode: brand pays famous artist to appear in a 30-second spot, submits as music.

How to detect: did the brand contribute to the **music itself** (commissioned a song, supported a tour, helped a niche scene)? Or just bought a famous artist's likeness?

When the native check fails, hard-cap Idea ≤ 60 and Execution ≤ 50.

---

## Patterns in Music winners

| Pattern | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| `original-track-as-the-idea` | Brand commissions an original song that lives beyond the campaign |
| `artist-collab-deep` | Brand × artist creating product together (Beats, Adidas, Apple Music sponsored EPs) |
| `genre-scene-amplification` | Brand supports an underserved music scene (regional, emerging, niche) |
| `music-tech-platform` | Brand creates a tool/platform for musicians (loops, plugins, distribution) |
| `cultural-moment-via-music` | Music-led moment that drives cultural conversation |
| `concert-as-activation` | Live experience design with real audience numbers |
