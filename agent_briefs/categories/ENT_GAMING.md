## Native-to-category check

Failure mode: brand pastes its logo into Fortnite / Roblox and submits as Gaming.

How to detect: did the brand earn its place in the gaming experience? Did players choose to engage with it? Did streamers feature it organically? Or was it just paid logo placement?

When the native check fails, hard-cap Idea ≤ 60 and Execution ≤ 50.

## Patterns in Gaming winners

| Pattern | Example shape |
|---|---|
| `branded-game-mode` | Brand becomes a playable mode (Wendy's "Keeping Fortnite Fresh") |
| `streamer-partnership-organic` | Streamer integrates brand into their style genuinely |
| `e-sports-sponsorship-with-creative` | Tournament sponsorship that brings creative leverage |
| `gaming-culture-tribute` | Real love-letter to a gaming community, not extractive |
| `gameplay-as-the-message` | The gameplay mechanic IS the brand message |
| `cross-platform-meta` | Same idea across multiple gaming platforms |
