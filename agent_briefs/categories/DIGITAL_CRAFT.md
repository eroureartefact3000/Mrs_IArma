## Native-to-category check

Failure mode: a brand-awareness microsite with off-the-shelf craft submitted as Digital Craft.

How to detect: does the board show **specific craft technique** (custom WebGL shaders, micro-interaction frames, motion easing curves, type-as-interface)? Or just screenshots of a generic site?

When the native check fails, hard-cap Idea ≤ 60 and Execution ≤ 50.

## Patterns in Digital Craft winners

| Pattern | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| `motion-as-narrative` | The transitions tell the story (Apple, Linear) |
| `webgl-as-the-experience` | Custom 3D / shader-driven hero scenes |
| `generative-art-as-identity` | Brand visual generated procedurally (NFT-era, AI art) |
| `accessibility-first-as-craft` | Inclusive design treated as a craft achievement |
| `data-viz-as-the-product` | Interactive data visualisations as the brand artefact |
| `AR-as-product-demo` | AR overlay that demonstrates a product feature |
