# Category brief — Industry Craft Lions

> Per-category specifics for Cannes Lions Industry Craft: axis weights, sub-category landscape, judging emphasis. Self-contained, no references to other files.

---

## What Industry Craft celebrates

The Industry Craft Lions celebrate the **craft disciplines that bring advertising ideas to life**: illustration, typography, photography, art direction, copywriting, music, motion. The board IS the craft itself.

Per the entry kit, **Execution dominates by a wide margin** — idea and strategy are subordinate to craft excellence.

## Axis weights (use these for `weighted_score`)

| Axis | Weight | Why this weight in Industry Craft |
|---|---|---|
| **Idea** | **0.20** | Concept matters — but in service of craft. |
| **Strategy** | **0.10** | Light sanity-check on brand-mission fit. |
| **Execution** | **0.55** | **Dominant weight by far.** Craft IS the category. |
| **Impact** | **0.15** | Industry recognition, craft-press pickup. Business outcomes less central here than for Direct/Media. |


## Native-to-category check (Industry Craft-specific)

Industry Craft's signature failure mode: **a campaign that's strong on concept but ordinary on craft**, submitted because the agency thinks the idea is special.

Common offenders:
- A **clever concept** with stock-photo execution and template typography
- A **PR-driven campaign** that picked up press for the idea, not the craft
- **Heavy-CGI work** where the technology does the work, not artisan craft
- **Brand identity work** more suited to Design Lions

How to detect:
- Read the board. Can you **identify the specific craft technique** being celebrated (the type system, the photographer's lighting, the illustration style)?
- Is the craft visible at the resolution of the board? Hand-drawn line quality, type construction details, photographic micro-detail should show up.
- Industry Craft work invites the viewer to look closely. If the work depends on context (case film) to land, it's probably not craft-first.

When the work fails the native check:
- In **extraction**: still extract normally
- In **Rationale**: **hard-cap Idea ≤ 60 and Execution ≤ 50** and call this out explicitly.

Example phrasing:

> *"Submitted to Industry Craft but the work is concept-led with workmanlike execution — the jury had no specific craft technique to single out for celebration."*

## Patterns commonly seen in Industry Craft winners

| Pattern | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| `signature-illustration-style` | Recognisable illustration approach used across a campaign (linework, painting, collage) |
| `type-system-as-craft` | Custom typeface or kerning approach that IS the work |
| `photography-as-narrative` | Photographer's eye carrying the entire campaign (e.g. Annie Leibovitz cinematic series) |
| `craft-of-restraint` | The discipline NOT to over-design — empty space, single-image hero |
| `material-craft` | Tactile / physical craft (handmade props, sculptural work, paper engineering) |
| `sound-design-as-the-idea` | Sound design or music that carries the brand message |
| `motion-craft-mastery` | Animation, editing, or motion design where the technique itself wins the prize |

## Quick checklist before submitting a rationale for Industry Craft

- [ ] Strategy stays strictly WHY (brand mission fit, brief-to-craft fit) — no craft leaking in
- [ ] Execution stays strictly HOW (technique, mastery, micro-detail) — no brand mission leaking in
- [ ] Native-to-category check applied (if craft is workmanlike, hard-cap Idea/Execution)
- [ ] **Specific craft technique named** in the Execution rationale (typography, illustration approach, photographic style, etc.)
- [ ] All percentages have a "of what?" check (RIGOUR pattern 1)
- [ ] Press claims framed as entrant-reported (RIGOUR pattern 2)
- [ ] No vague superlatives without an immediate concrete justification (RIGOUR pattern 4)
- [ ] Cultural / linguistic scope named precisely when relevant (RIGOUR pattern 5)
- [ ] Synthesis-style fields are observational, never advisory
- [ ] Weighted score = 0.20·Idea + 0.10·Strategy + 0.55·Execution + 0.15·Impact
- [ ] `tier_consistency` set honestly
