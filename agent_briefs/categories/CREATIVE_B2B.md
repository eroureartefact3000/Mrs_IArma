## Native-to-category check

Failure mode: a consumer brand campaign rebadged as B2B because the brand also sells to businesses.

How to detect: is the audience **explicitly a business decision-maker** (CIO, CFO, procurement, ops lead)? Is there a B2B-specific channel (LinkedIn, trade media, sales enablement, ABM)? Is the impact measured in pipeline, not consumer impressions?

When the native check fails, hard-cap Idea ≤ 60 and Impact ≤ 50.

## Patterns in B2B winners

| Pattern | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| `category-disruption-rebrand` | Brand repositioning that shifts how the category sells |
| `ABM-creative-at-scale` | Narrow-target ABM with disproportionate hit rate |
| `B2B-mascot-or-character` | Distinctive brand asset for a typically-boring category |
| `sales-enablement-as-creative` | Tools / collateral the sales team actually uses |
| `data-led-B2B-content` | Original research generating earned coverage in trade press |
| `thought-leadership-platform` | Brand owns a category conversation |

- [ ] Strategy strictly WHY (decision-maker identified, insight rooted in B2B truth)
- [ ] Execution strictly HOW (B2B channel choice, sales-enablement integration)
- [ ] Native-to-category check applied — **B2B audience explicitly targeted?**
- [ ] **Pipeline / lead / deal metric named** in Impact (not consumer impressions)
- [ ] RIGOUR patterns 1-5 applied
